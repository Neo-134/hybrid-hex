INTRODUCTION

The Hybrid Diamond Lattice Heat Exchanger (HyDiL-HEx) is an innovative heat exchanger design that combines the advantage of a Parallel-flow heat exchanger, having the highest initial heat transfer rate, and that of a Counter-flow heat exchanger, having the highest overall thermal efficiency into a single device. It does so by having a Parallel-flow heat exchanger design seamlessly transition into a Counter-flow heat exchanger, therefore, preserving the benefits of both. It uses a lattice geometry that improves heat transfer in compact structures.

ENGINEERING BACKGROUND

How Current Heat Exchanger Flows Work

Parallel Flow: Both fluids enter at the same end and move in the same direction. The large initial temperature difference gives rapid heat transfer at the inlet, making it fast, but the temperature difference drops quickly along the length, leading to only partial heat transfer.

Counter Flow: Fluids enter from opposite ends and move in opposite directions. The temperature difference is maintained more uniformly along the exchanger, so heat transfer occurs more gradually and thoroughly, resulting in slower but more complete heat transfer.

  • Parallel Flow is Fast, but Incomplete
  • Counter Flow is Slow, but Complete

How The Hybrid Heat Exchanger Flow Works

In the Hybrid Flow, both fluids first enter at the same end and move in the same direction. The large initial temperature difference gives rapid heat transfer at the inlet. This is the Parallel half. As the temperature difference quickly drops and parallel flow's heat transfer advantage vanishes, the partially cooled Hot fluid transitions into the Counter-flow half, where it gradually exchanges its residual heat with oncoming Cold fluid, till it achieves the maximum required heat transfer

  • Hybrid Flow is both Fast and Complete
  • Hybrid Flow Exchangers are Compact

How the TPMS Lattice Geometry Works

The hot and cold flow passages of the HyDiL-HEx are generated using the Diamond TPMS lattice. Imagine a sponge made of smooth, twisting tunnels instead of holes. Fluid flows through these interconnected passages the way water moves through a sponge, constantly changing direction and touching a large surface area, but without both fluids mixing. This continuous contact using curved walls lets the fluids move smoothly, improving heat transfer in a compact space.

  • Lattices Minimize Pressure Loss
  • Lattices Maximize Internal Surface Area

OUR SOLUTION

The Hybrid Diamond Lattice Heat Exchanger

The Hybrid Diamond Lattice Heat Exchanger (HyDiL-HEx) is an original design that combines the benefits of parallel-flow (initial heat transfer rates due to large inlet temperature differences) and counter-flow heat exchangers (superior overall thermal efficiency by allowing outlet temperatures to closely approach opposing inlet temperatures) into a single, compact system. The HyDiL-HEx seamlessly transitions from a parallel-flow configuration to a counter-flow configuration along its flow length, thereby preserving both advantages. This improves heat transfer efficiency by about 35% compared to counterflow heat exchangers with similar surface area.

Heat Exchanger Geometry

Its hot and cold flow passages are generated using the Diamond TPMS lattice. Thewse are 3D cellular structures that repeat periodically in three dimensions essentially forming smooth, twisting tunnels and interconnected passages through which both fluids move, constantly changing direction and touching a large surface area, but without fluids mixing. This continuous contact using curved walls lets the fluids move efficiently, improving heat transfer in a compact space.

APPLICATIONS

Electricity Generation from Gas Flares

Instead of letting the flare burn off energy into the air, the hot exhaust is captured and passed through the HyDiL-HEx and recycled to generate steam which drives a micro-turbine to generate electricity.

Conversion of Gas to Combined Cycle Powerplant

After the gas turbine produces power, its hot exhaust is passed through the HyDiL-HEx and recycled to generate steam for a second turbine, allowing the plant to extract more energy from the same fuel.
